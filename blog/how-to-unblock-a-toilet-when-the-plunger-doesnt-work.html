How to unblock a toilet when the plunger doesn't work?

When your toilet is clogged and the plunger has failed, you might find yourself in a sticky situation. This is especially true if you have pets at home, or live in a house with other people who might be upset when they find out about your situation. But don't worry! With the right tools and techniques, you can unclog any toilet, no matter how stubborn it is. Read on to learn more about how you can unclog a toilet when the plunger can't help you.Unclogging a toilet when the plunger fails will take some time and effort on your part. You'll likely have to use a toilet auger or other cleaning tools to break up the clog so you can flush it away. Before you start unclogging your toilet, you'll need to shut off the water supply so you don't risk flooding your home. Once the toilet is unplugged and you've turned off the water, you'll need to use a toilet auger to dig out the clog. You'll then need to unclog the toilet with a plunger after you've broken up the clog with the auger.

Check the trap

If the toilet is clogged, the first thing you should do is check the trap. The trap is a U-shaped piece of plumbing that's located below the toilet in the floor of your home. Clogged toilets often occur when food waste and other debris gets caught in the trap.If you can't see the trap from above the toilet, you'll need to remove the toilet seat to check it. You can do this by unscrewing the bolts on the underside of the toilet bowl. Once you've removed the bolts, you can pull the bowl off the toilet. From there, you can see the trap.If the trap is clogged, you'll need to clean it out and unclog it before you can unplug the toilet and use a toilet auger to break up the clog.

Use a toilet auger

If the toilet is still plugged after you've checked the trap and failed to break it up with the plunger, you'll need to use a toilet auger to break up the clog. To use a toilet auger, you'll need to shut off the water supply to the toilet. You can do this by turning the water supply shut off valve located either on the wall or in the floor near the toilet.Next, you'll need to remove the toilet seat and flush the toilet a few times to clear any debris that might be blocking the water from flowing down the drain. Once the toilet is clear, you'll need to plug the toilet with a plug that's attached to the end of the auger.You'll then need to push the auger down the drain of the toilet until you feel it hit a clog. Once you've hit the clog, you'll need to turn the auger in the opposite direction to break up the clog.

Use a plunger

If you've tried the toilet auger and failed to break up the clog, you'll need to use a plunger to break up the clog. Before you use the plunger, you'll need to shut off the water supply to the toilet. Once the toilet is shut off, you'll need to flush the toilet a few times to clear any debris that might be blocking the water from flowing down the drain.Next, you'll need to remove the toilet seat, plug the toilet, and push the plunger down the sink to break up the clog. After you've broken up the clog, you can flush the toilet again to unplug the toilet.

Bottom line

As you can see, there are a variety of ways you can unclog a toilet when the plunger fails and cannot help.
