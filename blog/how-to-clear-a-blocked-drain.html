How to clear a blocked drain?

A kitchen sink can quickly become a nightmare with a build-up of food, grease and other nasty things that can easily turn into a clogged and messy drain if you don’t keep an eye on it. If your kitchen sink starts to back up with unpleasant-smelling water, it’s probably time to clear a clogged kitchen sink – before it gets even worse and makes your home or business smell like a sewer! It doesn’t take much for a kitchen sink’s drain to become clogged; even a tiny piece of food can cause it to back up. Fortunately, most clogged kitchen sinks are easy to clear. It’s just a matter of finding the right tools and following these steps:

Step 1: Check for Clogged Drains

If your kitchen sink starts to back up, it’s a good idea to check for a clogged drain before taking any action. There are a variety of ways to do this, including using a plunger or a snake to break up the clog. If you’re using a plunger, be sure to only use a small one, as a large one can cause more damage than good. Using a snake is another way to check for clogged drains. Be sure to follow the instructions on the package carefully before attempting to use it, and make sure that you don’t turn the faucet on while the clog is being cleared.

Step 2: Use a Plunger

If your kitchen sink begins to back up, the first thing to try is a plunger. You can buy a basic plunger at your local hardware store, or you can make your own DIY plunger. If you’re using a homemade plunger, be sure to choose a container that you can easily remove from the sink once the clog has been removed. It’s best to try to clear the clog without turning on the faucet, as this will prevent you from damaging pipes and appliances further down the line.

Step 3: Use a Snake

A snake can help you to clear a clogged kitchen sink quickly, without causing any damage. To use a snake, simply follow the instructions on the package. Be sure to unplug any appliances in the sink to avoid risking damage from an appliance power cord getting tangled in the snake. If you’re using a power cord-operated snake, be sure to unplug the appliance.

Step 4: Try a Plastic Shovel

If a plunger and a snake fail to clear your kitchen sink, you can try using a plastic shovel. This tool can break up a clog and clear your kitchen sink quickly. If you have a large clog, you may want to try using a shovel two or three times in a row. This will help to break up the clog and allow water to pass through again.

Step 5: Try a Toothbrush

If a shovel, a plunger, a snake and a plastic shovel all fail to clear your kitchen sink, try using a toothbrush to manually unclog the sink. This will allow water to flow through the sink again, and you can check for a clog further down the line. It’s important to remember that the teeth of a toothbrush are not designed to go down a pipe.

Step 6: Break the Dirt Down With Sand

If all the previous steps fail, break the dirt down with sand. This can help to prevent a clog from re-forming in the future, and it will also help to keep your kitchen sink clean. Be sure to wear gloves and eye protection when using sand, and remember to dispose of sand properly when you’re finished.

Step 7: Clear the Drain with Chemicals

If all of the above steps fail to clear your kitchen sink, you can try to clean the sink with chemicals. Be sure to wear gloves and eye protection when using these chemicals, and remember to dispose of them properly when you’re finished. To use chemicals to clear your kitchen sink, mix a solution of baking soda and vinegar and pour it into the sink. Allow this to sit for around 30 minutes before turning the faucet on and letting it run for another 30 minutes.

Step 8: Get Help from a Professional

If all of the above steps fail to clear your kitchen sink, you may want to consider getting help from a professional. It’s important to note that you’re legally responsible for the repair costs of a clogged kitchen sink, so it’s recommended to call a professional before you start digging. If you decide to hire a contractor, you should be sure to check for licensing and insurance before starting any work.
