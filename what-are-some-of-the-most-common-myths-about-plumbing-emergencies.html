What are some of the most common myths about plumbing emergencies?

When it comes to plumbing, there are a lot of myths out there. People believe that only old buildings have problems with plumbing, that you need to call a plumber or contractor for every little issue, and that you need to be present at the same time as your plumber for a thorough inspection. That last one is especially funny because most people don’t realize you can do a thorough inspection with just a video camera and a smartphone! However, most of the myths surrounding plumbing are based on misconceptions about what a plumbing emergency actually is. There are plenty of circumstances where you need a plumber, but a plumbing emergency is a bit different. The term ‘emergency’ is subjective, but it generally means something unexpected and imminent. Let’s tackle some of the myths surrounding these types of situations, and see what actually needs to happen so that you don’t have an unfortunate event in the future.

You don’t need a plumber if you notice a leak in your water supply pipe.

If you see a small drip or a few drips from a water pipe, it’s likely condensation. The source of water leaks that occur in your home are typically on the inside of the supply pipes, not the outside. The water supply pipes are located in the walls, and they’re often hidden behind drywall or another material. If you see a drip, don’t panic. It’s likely condensation from a nearby appliance or something in the room you’re in has a higher humidity than the rest of the house. You can check your water meter to see if the water is being used correctly. If it’s off, that could be a sign of a larger issue, and you should call a plumber as soon as possible.

You don’t need a plumber if you smell sewage in your home.

If you start smelling sewage in your home, you should contact a plumber. While this can sometimes happen if your pipes are old or you have a clog, it’s more often a sign that there’s something wrong with your sewage system. If sewage is coming into your home, it’s usually because there’s some sort of blockage in the sewer line, and it needs to be addressed immediately. If you smell sewage and you’re not sure what’s causing it, contact a plumber immediately. If it’s sewage, your home will have to be deodorized and then sanitized after it’s been treated.

You don’t need a plumber if you have a broken toilet handle.

Most people think that you need to call a plumber for every single toilet handle that breaks. While there are a few exceptions, you typically only need a plumber to replace a broken toilet handle. If your toilet is leaking, or if you have a leaking toilet, you need to call a plumber to fix it. Many people think that they can fix these issues themselves by simply replacing the parts, but this is a common misconception. If you have a broken toilet handle, but you think you can repair it without calling a plumber, you’re likely going to end up with a bigger problem. If you try to repair the issue and it doesn’t work, you’ll likely have to replace the entire toilet. This adds unnecessary expense to an issue that could have been avoided.

You don’t need a plumber if you have frozen pipes.

If you have a frozen pipe, you may assume that you need a plumber to fix it. However, most frozen pipes aren’t caused by a problem with the pipes themselves. They’re typically the result of a broken pipe outside your home, and they’re usually the result of a broken or damaged frost guard on a faucet. If you have a frozen pipe, it’s recommended that you avoid thawing the pipe by putting hot water through it. This can help you avoid breaking the pipe. If you do break your pipes, you’ll likely need to replace them if you don’t have a warranty. Frozen pipes can be dangerous, and they can cause damage to your home.

Conclusion

In general, you don’t need to call a plumber for every issue that you have with your plumbing. However, there are a few issues that will require the help of a professional. If you have an emergency situation, you’ll want to call a plumber as soon as possible. If you notice a problem before it becomes an emergency, you’ll still want to call a plumber, but you may be able to address it on your own.
